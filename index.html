03 · THE VENUE

BALLERS Bangkok.

A premium football venue in central Bangkok — two pitches in parallel, full Performance Center, locker rooms, hydration zone and a built-in Spanish football identity that anchors the day around the right cultural codes.

Located in Huai Kwang, Pracha Uthit Road 23 — accessible for the Spanish, Thai and international communities the SPTCC convenes. Recovery area, mobility studio, on-site staff and full event-day operations.

BALLERS Bangkok is built as the year-round Spanish football home in the city. The tournament is the natural amplification of that identity — and the venue is purpose-fit for the scale of the event.

Spanish football identity,
tournament-wide.

07 · SPECIAL PRIZES

The trophies behind the trophy.

Beyond the team standings, four individual awards in the spirit of Spanish football — three for the moments of brilliance, one for the lighter side of the game.

01
MVP
Best Player
The standout of the tournament — the player who shaped the day across both phases. Voted by coaches, refs and partners.
Spanish football tradition: the player you remember a year later.
02
PICHICHI
Top Scorer
Most goals across group stage and finals combined. Tiebreak: fewest minutes per goal. The hunter.
After Rafael Moreno "Pichichi" — Athletic Bilbao legend, 1900s.
03
ZAMORA
Best Goalkeeper
Fewest goals conceded across all matches played. The wall behind the wall.
After Ricardo Zamora — Spain national-team keeper, 1920s-30s.
04
LIMÓN
Tough Day Award
For the player who suffered the most on the pitch — picked by their own teammates. Worn with honour. Taken with humour.
Spanish football tradition: el trofeo limón. Football laughing at itself.
